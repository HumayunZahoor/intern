Imran Khan

Former Prime Minister of Pakistan

Imran Khan is a Pakistani politician, former cricketer, and philanthropist who served as the 22nd Prime Minister of Pakistan from 2018 to 2022. Before entering politics, he was internationally renowned as one of the greatest all-rounders in cricket history and led Pakistan to their first and only Cricket World Cup victory in 1992.

Early Life

Imran Khan was born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan. He attended Aitchison College in Lahore and later went to the Royal Grammar School Worcester in England. Khan graduated from Keble College, Oxford, in 1975, where he studied Philosophy, Politics, and Economics.

Cricket Career

Imran Khan made his Test debut for Pakistan in 1971 and quickly established himself as one of the world's leading fast bowlers and all-rounders. His most significant achievement came in 1992 when he captained Pakistan to World Cup glory. He retired from cricket in 1992 as one of the sport's legends.

Political Career

In 1996, Imran Khan founded the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party. After years of struggle, PTI emerged as a major political force in the 2013 elections. Khan's persistence paid off in 2018 when PTI won the general elections, and he became the Prime Minister of Pakistan.

During his tenure, Khan focused on anti-corruption measures, economic reforms, and social welfare programs. His government initiated several projects aimed at improving the country's infrastructure, healthcare, and education systems.

Legacy

Imran Khan's legacy extends beyond politics. He founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital & Research Centre, Pakistan's largest tertiary care cancer hospital, named after his mother who died of cancer. Additionally, he established Namal University to provide quality education to the youth of Pakistan.

Khan remains a significant figure in Pakistan and worldwide, celebrated for his contributions to cricket, his philanthropic efforts, and his political career.
